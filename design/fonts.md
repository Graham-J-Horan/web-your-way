Here are the top 10 most common website fonts that work across industries — chosen for readability, browser support, and professional neutrality:

---

## 1. **Inter**
The current modern default. Designed specifically for screens, highly readable at any size. Used by Shopify, Mozilla, and thousands of SaaS companies. Works for tech, corporate, creative, and service businesses.

---

## 2. **Open Sans**
One of the most widely used sans-serifs on the web. Neutral, friendly, and extremely legible. A safe choice for almost any industry — professional services, healthcare, education, non-profits.

---

## 3. **Roboto**
Google's system font. Clean, geometric, slightly mechanical. Used across Google products and Android. Ideal for tech, engineering, and modern corporate sites.

---

## 4. **Montserrat**
Bold, geometric, and distinctive. Has a strong presence at large sizes. Popular for headings in creative fields — fashion, design, architecture, agencies. Less ideal for long body text.

---

## 5. **Poppins**
Rounded, modern geometric sans-serif. Friendly and approachable. Widely used in lifestyle, beauty, wellness, and small business sites. Best for headings and short blocks of text.

---

## 6. **Lato**
Warm, professional, and balanced. Works across both headings and body text. Used by Airbnb and many corporate brands. Suited to professional services, consulting, and hospitality.

---

## 7. **Nunito**
Rounded, friendly, and highly readable. Less corporate than Roboto or Open Sans. Common in wellness, childcare, education, and creative small businesses.

---

## 8. **Source Sans Pro / Source Sans 3**
Adobe's open-source sans-serif. Clean, professional, and highly legible. Works for government, education, enterprise, and technical documentation.

---

## 9. **Merriweather**
The most popular serif for body text on screens. Traditional, trustworthy, and readable. Used for editorial, publishing, law firms, and any business wanting a classic, established feel.

---

## 10. **Playfair Display**
High-contrast serif, typically used for headings only. Elegant and sophisticated. Common in luxury, fashion, wedding, and high-end service businesses.

---

## Quick Selection Guide

| Industry | Recommended Fonts |
|----------|------------------|
| Tech / SaaS | Inter, Roboto, Source Sans |
| Professional Services | Open Sans, Lato, Merriweather |
| Creative / Design | Montserrat, Poppins, Playfair Display |
| Lifestyle / Wellness | Poppins, Nunito, Lato |
| Hospitality / Travel | Lato, Open Sans, Playfair Display |
| Editorial / Publishing | Merriweather, Source Sans |

---

## Important Note
Most commercial websites use **2 fonts maximum** — one for headings and one for body text. Using more than two typically looks inconsistent and harms user experience.
